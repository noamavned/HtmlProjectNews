Scientists Discover New Jellyfish Species That Glows Like a Neon Sign

Marine biologists uncover a glowing jellyfish with unique bioluminescent patterns

Scientists exploring the Gulf of Mexico have announced the discovery of a new jellyfish species that emits a neon-blue glow.

The species, known as Aurelia luminis, was first detected at a depth of nearly 1,200 feet, where light is scarce.

Researchers say the jellyfish's unusual blinking pattern may help confuse predators or attract prey.

Samples collected during the expedition revealed genetic markers not found in related species.

The discovery highlights how little is known about deep-sea life and how many mysteries remain uncovered.
