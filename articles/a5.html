Study Shows Dogs Can Recognize Lies — And They Don’t Forget

Researchers find dogs react differently when they know they’ve been misled

A new study from Kyoto University suggests dogs can detect when a person intentionally misleads them.

In the experiment, researchers repeatedly pointed dogs toward containers with and without food.

When scientists deliberately indicated empty containers, dogs quickly learned not to follow their gestures.

Later, even when the person was truthful, the dogs showed hesitation or refused to trust them.

The results indicate dogs possess a higher level of social intelligence than previously believed.
