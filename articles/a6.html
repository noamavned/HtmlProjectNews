City Tests New “Quiet Pavement” to Reduce Traffic Noise by 40%

A new road surface aims to make urban streets significantly quieter

A mid-size city in Denmark has begun testing a new type of asphalt designed to absorb sound rather than reflect it.

Early reports show noise levels dropping by nearly 40% in test areas compared to traditional pavement.

Drivers reported no change in traction or performance, suggesting the material is safe for everyday use.

City officials say the technology could be rolled out across busy intersections if tests continue to be successful.

Researchers hope the quiet pavement will help reduce noise pollution in densely populated areas.
